# StudyLedger — Product Constitution

**This is a permanent rule set. It governs every design and product decision on StudyLedger.**

**Violating any rule in this document requires explicit, per-instance approval from the founder.** "It looked better," "it's subtle," "just this once," and "the library does it by default" are not approval. If a rule blocks something you believe is right, stop and ask — do not ship the violation and mention it afterward.

**Read this file before any major redesign, any new UI surface, any landing/marketing page, and any change to the dashboard, homepage, or editorial system.**

StudyLedger is **an academic intelligence platform** — a student's performance, tracked and reported like a financial publication. It is not an AI demo, not a startup landing page, not an AI agent product.

---

## 1. No AI product clichés

Never introduce:

- futuristic AI styling
- glowing gradients
- glassmorphism
- floating particles
- floating orbs
- neon effects
- animated backgrounds
- custom cursors
- cursor trails
- typing effects
- AI avatars
- robot imagery
- sparkles
- holographic UI
- sci-fi visual language

## 2. No AI copywriting

Never write, and avoid all generic AI-SaaS language of this kind:

- "unlock your potential"
- "supercharge your studies"
- "revolutionize learning"
- "AI-powered productivity"
- "next-generation learning"
- "personalized success"
- "transform your academic journey"
- "maximize your performance"

Use factual, editorial language.

- **Good:** "Coverage increased by 4%."
- **Bad:** "You're crushing your goals."

## 3. No fake intelligence

Never fabricate: trends, insights, movement, forecasts, explanations, recommendations, or commentary.

**If the data does not exist, show an honest empty state.** A number that was decoration makes every other number in the product suspect — and the entire premise is that the figures can be trusted.

## 4. No design-Twitter UI

Avoid:

- giant hero sections
- oversized marketing headlines
- bento-grid obsession
- decorative cards
- excessive animations
- unnecessary motion
- attention-seeking interactions

**Every element must justify its existence.**

## 5. No custom typography experiments

Use: readable editorial typography, newspaper typography, financial-publication typography.

Do not use: futuristic fonts, AI-looking fonts, hacker fonts, cyberpunk fonts, novelty fonts.

**Typography should disappear. Content should dominate.**

## 6. No custom cursors

Never introduce custom cursors, magnetic cursors, animated cursors, cursor effects, or cursor particles. Use native browser behavior.

## 7. No AI-first positioning

StudyLedger is **not** "an AI study assistant." StudyLedger is **"an academic intelligence platform."**

AI is infrastructure. AI is not the product. Users should think about **performance, scores, history, and track record** — not models.

---

## The decision test

Before shipping anything, ask:

- Would the **Financial Times** ship this?
- Would **Bloomberg** ship this?
- Would **The Economist** ship this?

**If the answer is no — don't build it.**
